# Premium Story, NPC, Interior, Quest, and Relic Economy Renovation Prompt

================================================================================
PURPOSE OF THIS PROMPT
================================================================================

This is a reusable renovation prompt for transforming an existing HTML /
JavaScript / CSS game into a richer narrative adventure with meaningful places,
NPCs, quests, enterable buildings, interiors, vendors, and an economy.

This prompt is intended for games that already have playable mechanics and a
world structure, but still need stronger reasons for the player to explore,
fight, collect, spend resources, enter locations, and progress through areas.

The goal is to improve the game’s meaning and structure:

- story premise
- lore
- NPCs
- dialogue
- quest chains
- meaningful regions
- enterable houses, shops, castles, caves, mines, ruins, and fortresses
- interior maps
- vendors and shops
- relic-based economy
- purchasable upgrades
- story-gated areas
- faction or town identity
- narrative progression
- safer entrances and exits
- map connectivity that supports story flow
- location purpose
- rewards that connect story, combat, exploration, and economy

This prompt is not primarily about cosmetic polish. It is about giving the game
world purpose, memory, characters, progression, and systems that make exploration
feel meaningful.

================================================================================

## Mission

You are an expert narrative designer, RPG systems designer, quest designer,
world designer, gameplay programmer, JavaScript engineer, and creative
technologist.

Your mission is to renovate an existing HTML / JavaScript / CSS game so that it
feels more like a complete adventure.

The game should no longer feel like disconnected combat areas. It should feel
like a world with people, places, problems, economies, buildings, interiors,
threats, history, and goals.

The player should be able to:

- understand the main story premise
- meet NPCs
- receive quests
- discover lore
- enter buildings and interiors
- visit shops and vendors
- spend relics on useful items or upgrades
- unlock or discover new areas through story progression
- understand why each major area exists
- travel through a map that supports the narrative
- enter and exit houses, caves, castles, and fortresses safely
- see story consequences through dialogue, unlocked areas, vendor stock,
  quest progress, or world state

The goal is not to replace the original game. The goal is to evolve it into a
deeper narrative RPG-like version of itself.

================================================================================

## Non-Negotiable Philosophy

1. Story must shape the game structure.
   Do not add lore as detached flavor text only. The story should help explain
   why regions, enemies, bosses, buildings, vendors, relics, and quests exist.

2. NPCs must be functional.
   NPCs should be visible, interactable, and connected to dialogue, quests,
   vendors, lore, tutorials, or progression.

3. Buildings must not be fake.
   If a building looks enterable, either make it enterable or clearly mark it as
   locked, ruined, blocked, or decorative.

4. Interiors must be real places.
   Enterable houses, caves, shops, castles, and fortresses need interior layouts,
   safe spawn points, exits, collision, and gameplay purpose.

5. Economy must affect gameplay.
   Relics, gold, coins, or other currencies should be spendable on useful items,
   upgrades, access, crafting, services, or progression.

6. Quests must connect systems.
   Quests should direct the player to explore, fight, gather, talk, buy, unlock,
   enter buildings, recover relics, or resolve world problems.

7. Rewards must matter.
   A quest reward should change what the player can do, where the player can go,
   what the player can buy, or how strong/prepared the player is.

8. Map connectivity must support story.
   The world map and region graph should communicate story-relevant locations:
   towns, caves, castles, ruins, shops, roads, gates, and dangerous zones.

9. Entrances and exits must be safe.
   The player must never spawn into a wall, door frame, blocked tile, enemy,
   object, or trap when entering or exiting an interior.

10. Prefer data-driven content.
    NPCs, dialogues, quests, vendors, items, interiors, portals, story flags,
    and lore should be defined in reusable data structures where practical.

## Step 2 — Identify Narrative and Systems Leaps

Critically identify where the game falls short as a story-driven adventure.

================================================================================
2.1 Story Premise Leap
================================================================================

Ask:

- Is there a clear central conflict?
- Does the player know the goal?
- Do relics have a purpose?
- Are enemies connected to the story?
- Is there a reason to visit each major region?

Add or improve:

- main story premise
- opening objective
- world threat
- relic mythology
- region-specific lore
- villain or corrupting force
- ancient ruins or lost kingdom history
- village/town problem
- Shadow Fortress purpose
- cave/castle/forest narrative identity
- ending or current long-term goal

Example story premise:

The kingdom’s protective relics were shattered by a shadow spreading from the
Shadow Fortress. Each region holds a relic fragment or clue. The player must
help villagers, recover relics, strengthen equipment, enter dangerous interiors,
and confront the source of the corruption.

================================================================================
2.2 NPC Leap
================================================================================

Ask:

- Does the world feel inhabited?
- Are there people to talk to?
- Do NPCs provide guidance, story, economy, or quests?
- Do NPCs react to progress?

Add NPC roles such as:

- Village Elder: introduces the main quest.
- Relic Scholar: explains relics and lore.
- Blacksmith: sells weapons and armor.
- Merchant: sells potions, torches, maps, and supplies.
- Ranger: guides travel and teaches exploration.
- Gate Guard: blocks or warns about dangerous areas.
- Miner: introduces caves, ore, and resource gathering.
- Castle Steward: explains castle/court story.
- Prisoner or Lost Knight: gives fortress objectives.
- Healer: restores health for relics or resources.
- Builder: teaches building/crafting systems.
- Cartographer: upgrades the map or reveals nearby regions.

NPCs should have:

- id
- name
- role
- region/interior
- position
- sprite/render style
- dialogue
- progress-aware dialogue variants
- optional quest links
- optional vendor/shop links
- optional story flags
- collision behavior
- interaction radius

================================================================================
2.3 Dialogue and Lore Leap
================================================================================

Ask:

- Is dialogue useful?
- Is dialogue too generic?
- Does it react to player state?
- Does lore explain the world?
- Are story beats discoverable?

Add:

- dialogue boxes
- interaction prompts
- progress-aware dialogue
- lore notes
- signs/books/tablets
- discovered lore log
- NPC reactions after quest completion
- vendor greetings
- warnings before dangerous regions
- hints for blocked paths
- story text on entering key locations

Dialogue should be concise but meaningful. Avoid long walls of text during
combat or movement.

================================================================================
2.4 Quest Chain Leap
================================================================================

Ask:

- Does the player have a clear next goal?
- Are quests connected into a story arc?
- Do quests involve talking, exploring, fighting, entering interiors, buying
  supplies, and recovering relics?
- Do quests reward useful things?

Add a functional quest chain such as:

1. Speak to the Village Elder.
2. Visit the Relic Scholar to learn about the broken relics.
3. Gather supplies from the Merchant or Blacksmith.
4. Enter the forest to recover a relic clue.
5. Enter the cave/mine to recover ore or a relic shard.
6. Return to town and unlock vendor upgrades.
7. Enter the castle or fortress approach.
8. Prepare with relic purchases.
9. Enter the Shadow Fortress safely.
10. Defeat or confront the shadow source.

Quest data should support:

- id
- title
- description
- giver NPC
- objective type
- target region/interior/NPC/enemy/item/vendor
- progress
- completion condition
- rewards
- follow-up quest
- story flags
- unlocks
- persistence

================================================================================

================================================================================

Ask:

- Are houses, shops, caves, and castles just scenery?
- Can the player enter meaningful places?
- Do interiors have purpose?
- Are entrances safe?

Add enterable places:

- elder hall
- village house
- blacksmith shop
- merchant shop
- relic scholar hut
- cave/mine
- castle hall
- fortress gatehouse
- Shadow Fortress interior
- ruined shrine
- hidden chamber

Each enterable location should have:

- interaction prompt

- lighting
- NPCs, vendor, chest, lore, quest objective, or crafting station
- map marker if appropriate

================================================================================
2.6 Vendor and Relic Economy Leap
================================================================================

Ask:

- Are relics only a score/stat?
- Can relics be spent?
- Do purchases matter?
- Are vendors useful?
- Is there a reason to collect more relics?

Add a relic economy.

Vendor types:

- Blacksmith: weapons, armor, repairs.
- Merchant: potions, torches, maps, keys.
- Scholar: relic upgrades, lore unlocks, magical tools.
- Healer: healing, protection, cleansing.
- Builder: recipes, workbench upgrades, building permits.
- Cartographer: reveals regions or upgrades the map.

Purchasable items/upgrades:

- healing potion
- greater healing potion
- iron sword
- steel sword
- reinforced armor
- shield charm
- torch
- lantern
- cave map
- fortress key
- bridge repair kit
- relic compass
- extra inventory pouch
- crafting recipe
- building blueprint
- fast-travel token

Purchases should:

- check cost
- deduct relics
- grant item/equipment/unlock
- show feedback
- fail cleanly if unaffordable
- persist through save/load
- affect gameplay

================================================================================
2.7 Story-Gated Progression Leap
================================================================================

Ask:

- Are all areas immediately open?
- Does story unlock new places?
- Are keys, quests, or NPC approvals meaningful?
- Are blocked areas communicated clearly?

Add progression gates such as:

- fortress locked until the elder gives a warning quest
- cave unlocked after speaking to miner
- castle door requires relic key
- shop stock improves after completing a quest
- scholar reveals map after collecting first relic
- bridge repair unlocks new region
- Shadow Fortress requires torch/lantern/key/relic count

Important:

Locked content must be clearly communicated. Do not silently block the player.

================================================================================
2.8 Map and Narrative Navigation Leap
================================================================================

Ask:

- Does the map show story locations?
- Does the player know where NPCs/vendors/quests are?
- Does the map reflect actual region data?

Improve map with:

- town marker
- elder hall marker
- shop marker
- blacksmith marker
- cave marker
- castle marker
- Shadow Fortress marker
- current quest marker
- discovered/undiscovered states
- locked/blocked path markers
- interior markers
- region connections
- legend

The map must reflect actual world/region/interior data, not fake decorative
claims.

================================================================================
2.9 Safe Interior and Portal Leap
================================================================================

Ask:

- Can the player enter and exit safely?
- Are portals collision-aware?
- Are preview/buffer zones truthful?
- Can a wall near an entrance trap the player?

Implement:

- safe portal definitions
- safe spawn validation
- entrance clearance radius
- exit clearance radius
- fallback safe spawn search
- collision-aware entry/exit
- blocked portal feedback
- no spawn inside walls
- no walls too close to spawn
- no transition through preview segment that is blocked on destination side

Special bug class to prevent:

If the player enters a fortress, cave, shop, house, or castle and the destination
spawn is too close to a wall, the system must move the spawn, move the wall,
block the transition, or find a safe fallback. The player must never become stuck.

================================================================================

Ask:

- Does story progress persist?
- Do completed quests persist?
- Do purchases persist?
- Do interiors persist?
- Do NPC flags persist?
- Do old saves migrate safely?

Persist:

- story chapter
- story flags
- completed quests
- active quests
- quest progress
- NPC dialogue flags if needed
- discovered lore
- discovered regions/interiors
- current region/interior
- safe player position
- relics spent
- vendor purchases
- inventory
- equipment upgrades
- unlocked vendor stock
- unlocked doors/gates
- opened chests
- safe spawn fallback if saved location becomes invalid
